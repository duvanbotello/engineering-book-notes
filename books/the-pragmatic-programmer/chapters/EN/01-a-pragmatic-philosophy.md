# 01 - A pragmatic philosophy

> Quick navigation: [Core idea](#core-idea) · [Key concepts](#key-concepts) · [Actions](#actions) · [Related notes](#related-notes)

## Core idea

A pragmatic philosophy combines personal ownership, technical judgment, and team care. It is not about knowing everything, but about acting with honesty, solution focus, and continuous improvement.

## Key concepts

- **Professional responsibility:** own what we do, avoid external blame, and respond with solutions.
- **"The cat ate my source code":** admitting mistakes or gaps in knowledge strengthens trust and growth.
- **Software entropy:** technical disorder spreads when "broken windows" are tolerated.
- **Knowing when to stop:** avoiding over-engineering is also a quality decision.
- **Knowledge portfolio:** learn continuously and consistently, with diversity and technology risk management.
- **Critical thinking:** evaluate ideas by beneficiaries, context, and limits of application.
- **Effective communication:** clarity, audience, timing, listening, and consistency between code and documentation.

## Quotes (short only)

- "Focus on solutions, not excuses."
- "Do not leave broken windows."
- "You do not need to know everything, but you should know who knows."

## My interpretation

This chapter grounds a work ethic: being pragmatic is not improvising, it is taking ownership. When someone stops justifying errors and starts correcting with method, not only their code improves, but also their reputation and team impact.

## Practical lessons

- Ownership accelerates decisions because it reduces defensive discussions.
- Admitting "I do not know" unlocks learning and avoids ego-driven mistakes.
- Caring for design early prevents disorder from becoming culture.
- Learning a little, consistently, is more effective than learning a lot once.
- Communicating without the right context can ruin a good technical idea.

## Questions

- Am I explaining problems or solving them?
- Which "broken windows" am I tolerating today out of convenience?
- Is my learning plan consistent or only reactive?
- Can I adapt my message to the audience that needs it?

## Actions

- [ ] In every incident, record one solution action before any justification.
- [ ] Fix one small "broken window" per week in the codebase.
- [ ] Define a knowledge portfolio with realistic weekly goals.
- [ ] Before adopting a new practice, answer: who benefits, in what context it works, and when it does not.
- [ ] Review that relevant changes keep code and documentation aligned.
