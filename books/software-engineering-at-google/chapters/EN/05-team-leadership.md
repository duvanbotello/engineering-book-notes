# 05 - Team Leadership

## Core idea

An engineering team needs active leadership to move with focus. Effective leadership is not based on control, but on serving the team with humility, respect, and trust.

## Key concepts

- **Direction and responsibility:** without someone setting direction, the team loses pace and clarity.
- **Servant leadership:** the leader's role is to unblock the team, not to micromanage.
- **Culture of humility, respect, and trust:** these three foundations sustain technical collaboration.
- **Hiring to strengthen the team:** seek people who raise the bar and can even replace the leader.
- **Performance management through follow-up:** weekly meetings for underperformance and explicit milestone expectations.
- **Human factor in software:** the hardest part is not only code, but working with people and team dynamics.

## Quotes (short only)

- "A ship without a captain is just a floating waiting room."
- "The best leaders work to serve their team using humility, respect, and trust."
- "Resist the temptation to manage."

## My interpretation

The chapter's core message is that good leadership means creating conditions for better team performance without constant control. Leadership impact appears in cultural quality, clear expectations, and direct support when someone falls behind.

## Practical lessons

- Defining concrete milestones prevents ambiguous performance conversations.
- Frequent follow-up works better than late corrections.
- Hiring talent that challenges the leader improves team resilience.
- Trust does not replace high standards; it makes them more effective.

## Questions

- Am I leading to serve the team or to control execution?
- Are my success and failure expectations truly measurable through milestones?
- Do I have a clear support plan for underperforming team members?

## Actions

- [ ] Define explicit milestones for key sprint tasks.
- [ ] Schedule weekly follow-up with any member who needs close support.
- [ ] Review hiring practices to prioritize people who raise the team's level.
- [ ] Evaluate leadership decisions with humility, respect, and trust as criteria.
